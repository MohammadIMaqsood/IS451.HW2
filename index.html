Scarface

Scarface is a 1983 American crime drama film directed by Brian De Palma and written by Oliver Stone. It stars Al Pacino as Tony Montana, a Cuban immigrant who rises to power in Miami's drug cartel during the 1980s.

Tony Montana

Tony Montana is a ruthless and ambitious character who embodies the American Dream in a twisted and violent manner. His journey from a poor refugee to a powerful drug lord highlights themes of power, corruption, and the impact of crime on individuals and society. Despite his violent and often immoral actions, Tony Montana remains an iconic and complex figure in cinema history.
